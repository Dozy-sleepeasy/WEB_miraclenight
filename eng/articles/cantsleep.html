1. Keep a regular biorhythm
In general, what time do you go to bed and when do you wake up? When you go to sleep and wake up at the same time every day, your circadian rhythm is kept regular. The ability to fall asleep more quickly can be achieved by maintaining a consistent circadian rhythm.

2. Expose yourself to both light and darkness
Whether we are asleep or awake is influenced greatly by light and darkness. As soon as our bodies encounter light, they recognize that it is time to wake up, and when they are exposed to darkness, they recognize that it is time to sleep. The easiest way to fall asleep is to keep your room dark at night, and have plenty of light in the day to help keep you awake.

3. Take up yoga or meditation
Our bodies have difficulty falling asleep when we are stressed out. In contrast, yoga prevents and relieves stress and tension by utilizing breathing patterns and body movements. The practice of meditation increases the production of the hormone melatonin, which greatly enhances our ability to fall asleep. Using mindfulness to fall asleep assists you in staying focused on the present and reduces worry as you fall asleep.

4. Let go of your desire to sleep
In some instances, lying in bed and waiting to fall asleep can have the exact opposite effect of not being able to sleep. Stress and anxiety associated with not being able to sleep can lead us to negative thoughts and keep us from falling asleep. Alleviate the pressure of needing to sleep. If your mind is relaxed, it will be much easier for you to fall asleep.
