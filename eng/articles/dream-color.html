What is the color
of your dreams?

by Lucas
Let's recall a recent dream. How was the dream? Was it black and white? Was it in color?

The term "dream" pertains to a series of images, sounds, and sensations experienced in the midst of sleep. Therefore, dreams vary from person to person. The history of research on 'dream color' is very intricate among the various sensory elements that make up dreams. Considering that more than 60% of human cognition is accomplished through vision, so it‚Äôs only stating the obvious. An interesting finding was that black-and-white and colored dreams were significantly different according to the study's time frame.

A study conducted by Bentley 1) in 1915 found that 80% of participants reported having dreams of black and white imagery.

There were only 20% of respondents who reported having colored dreams. A research 2)conducted in1942 reported only 29% of respondents had colored dreams.

The dream color ratio, on the other hand, reverses dramatically in a study conducted in 1968. In a study conducted by Herman 3) in 1968, 69% of participants reported having colored dreams. This represents an increase of more than twofold. Several experts, including Herman, attribute this change to the arrival of color television.

Accordingly, the researchers conducted an experiment 4) in a second study by dividing the 'color TV generation' under the age of 25 into groups. The 'black-and-white TV generation' was divided into groups over 55 (based on the time of the study). Accordingly, 70% of people who grew up watching color television reported that they had colored dreams, and about 40% of people who grew up watching black and white television reported having colored dreams. Both generations have statistically significant differences. There is no doubt that both generations possess different amounts of memory and sleep cycles, but experts at the time concluded that color television had a significant impact on them.

Recent follow-up studies found that only 4% of respondents had black-and-white dreams. At this point, it may be safe to conclude that our dreams are influenced by media consumption.

What about your dreams? Why don't you observe your dreams tonight to determine what color they are?
