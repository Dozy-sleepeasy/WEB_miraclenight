Q. What are monaural beats?

Monaural beats are sound waves that are combined to synchronize your brain waves to a specific frequency. Miracle Night takes normal monaural beats to the next level and provides layered monaural beats that are optimized for inducing sleep. This patented algorithm can only be experienced with Miracle Night.

Q. How do monaural beats help with sleep?

The human brain produces five different types of waves: gamma (30-100 Hz), beta (12-30 Hz), alpha (8-12 Hz), theta (4-8 Hz), and delta (0.5-4 Hz). Monaural beats induce deep sleep by synchronizing our brain waves to those that are emitted during sleep, which ranges between alpha and delta.

Q. Are there any risks of monaural beats?

Numerous studies have consistently demonstrated the absence of risks associated with monaural beats. Nevertheless, it is essential to maintain a safe distance from devices when playing high-volume sounds to prevent any adverse impact on your hearing. Therefore, we strongly recommend adhering to our volume guide. Additionally, individuals who are epileptic or prone to seizures are advised against using our service.

Q. What does inaudible frequency mean?

Inaudible frequency refers to any frequency outside of the range that can be heard by humans. Therefore, monaural beats that are mixed with inaudible frequencies cannot be physically heard.

Q. Is listening to monaural beats with inaudible frequencies equally as effective at inducing sleep?

Yes, listening to monaural beats with inaudible frequencies produces the same brain wave effects. Q. What are the criteria for optimal volume settings?

Monaural beats can induce sleep when the volume reaches about 20 dB or more for the listener. 20 dB can be compared to a very small whispering sound. According to studies, monaural beats are effective in a wide range of volumes ranging from about 20 dB to 75 dB.

Q. I cannot hear the sounds that well when the volume is set to the recommended level. Should I turn up the volume?

If you have trouble hearing the sounds while in a very quiet (about 20dB) environment, this may be due to personal hearing conditions. Even if you have low sound sensitivity or poor hearing, the effect on your brain waves is still the same, so we recommend that you do not turn up the volume.

Q. The recommended volume is too loud for me. Should I turn down the volume?

If the volume is too loud for you, it may damage your hearing or disturb your sleep. We strongly recommend setting the volume to a low level where you can just barely hear the sounds.
